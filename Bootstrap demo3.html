Janis Joplin

Born in 1943 in Port Arthur, Texas, Janis Joplin became a blues sensation of the late 1960s and 1970s. Despite being bullied and rejected by her high school peers for her appearance and nonconformist lifestyle, she unapologetically continued to embrace the beauty of her passion and talent for blues music. She attended college in the Summer of 1960 and went on to continue her education at the University of Texas at Austin. However, she did not finish her studies and left the state of Texas in 1963. Four years later, in 1967, Janis Joplin ascended to notability at California's Monterey Pop Festival with her band, Big Brother and the Holding Company.
