Best known as the bassist and main lyricist of Pink Floyd, Roger Waters was born in 1943 in Surrey, England, where he and his family suffered from tumultuous exposure to World War II. In 1965, he founded Pink Floyd with the other initial members of the band: Syd Barrett, Nick Mason, and Richard Wright. In 1973, the band released The Dark Side of The Moon, a staple album in the history of rock and music.
