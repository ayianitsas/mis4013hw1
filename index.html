Songs of the 1970s!

"Bohemian Rhapsody" by Queen is undoubetly one of the most influential songs of the 1970s. It was released in the mid-defining year of the period on Queen's fourth studio album, A Night At The Opera, and serves as a timeless rock classic with its complex structure.

"Another Brick in the Wall, Pt.2" by Pink Floyd is the fifth song on Pink Floyd's eleventh studio album, The Wall. Its chorus: We don't need no education, We don't need no thought control ... embodies an anarchist attitude towards the system and status quo, making it a relatable and unforgettable piece of the era.

"Me and Bobby McGee" by Janis Joplin rests as one of Joplin's most acknowledged tracks. Released on Pearl, the second and last studio album by Janis Joplin, it reflects a story of her and a lover named Bobby as they voyage from Southern to Western America. Personally, I find it a tune that bears close to me with its nostalgia.

"Dreams" by Fleetwood Mac is an absolute classic of the 1970s. It was released on Fleetwood Mac's eleventh studio album, Rumours, in 1977, a period of heavy turmoil for the group. As the band struggled in their relationships with one another, Dreams serves as a critical impression.
